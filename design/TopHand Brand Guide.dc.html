05 · Voice & posture

TopHand suggests. The farmer decides.

Warm, family-farm authentic, zero corporate gloss — made by a farmer who happens to build software. The hard rule below governs every line of copy.

The posture rule

TopHand suggests, surfaces, and supports. It never tells the farm what to do, and never implies automation without consent. The farmer makes the calls.

Say

  • “When a window opens, you see it.”
  • “TopHand suggests; you confirm.”
  • “It warns; it never blocks.”
  • “Advisory and editable — always yours.”

Don't say

  • “TopHand decides when to cut.”
  • “Automatically runs your farm.”
  • “Set it and forget it.”
  • Enterprise jargon, ALL-CAPS shouting

06 · App ↔ web alignment

Align at the token layer, not the component layer.

The marketing site carries the seed-catalog brand; the app runs on Google Material Design for usability and velocity. Don't force Material onto the site or the slab-serif look into dense UI. Instead, share identity tokens and let layout differ.

Identical in both

  • The mark and the domain icon set (ship once as shared SVGs)
  • Harvest Gold as the single accent, used sparingly
  • IBM Plex Mono for all data — condition gates, acreage, dates
  • The “window opens” logic and the posture-rule voice

Follow Material (let differ)

  • Density, elevation, and shadows
  • Navigation chrome — FABs, nav bars, chips, menus
  • Motion and state transitions
  • Form controls and component anatomy
